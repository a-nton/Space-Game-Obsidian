# Blathri Mor (Petal Giant)

#canon

**Biome:** [[Elderwood Highlands]]
**Tier:** Elite (appears in large numbers)
**Behaviour type:** Hostile (with unique mechanic)

---

## Overview

Massive walking flower colossi. Towering creatures that slowly patrol the Highlands in large numbers. Technically killable with weak-point targeting and a prolonged fight, but direct combat is heavily discouraged. The defining enemy of the area.

## Ecology

Autotroph. The dominant species in the Elderwood Highlands. No predator-prey dynamics here — the Petal Giants are the ecology. [[Seed Dancers]] accompany them and sing alongside them.

## Behaviour — Red Light, Green Light

The core mechanic: periodically, all Petal Giants stop, hold hands, and begin singing. During the singing phase, the player can move freely. When the music quiets down, they stop singing and look around. The player must hide. If spotted during the silent phase, the Petal Giants attack and kill instantly.

This creates a rhythm of movement and stillness that defines the entire Highlands experience.

## Attacks & Abilities

Lethal on contact during aggro. The threat is not any individual attack but the sheer number of them and the punishment for being seen.

## Weaknesses

Weak points exist for a prolonged killable fight, but the numbers make combat impractical. The intended approach is stealth and reading the rhythm.

## Visuals

Towering flower colossi. Should be beautiful and terrifying simultaneously. The hand-holding singing animation should feel eerily gentle for something that kills on sight.

## Audio

The singing is the core gameplay element. It must be clearly audible, rhythmically predictable after the player learns it, and atmospherically haunting. The transition from singing to silence should be abrupt enough to trigger urgency.

## Design Notes

This mechanic is the Elderwood Highlands' identity. It combines the area's themes — beauty, danger, and the [[The Beholder|Beholder's]] presence in the sky above — into a single gameplay loop. The player is hiding from giants while something unknowable watches from the sky. The stealth under cosmic observation is the point.
