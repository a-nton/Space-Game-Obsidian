# Dealgshiul (Thorn Ambusher)

#canon

**Biome:** [[Verdant Expanse]], [[Knotroot Canopy]]
**Tier:** Standard
**Behaviour type:** Hostile

---

## Overview

Low-standing quadrupedal ambush predator made of thorny vines and bark-like spine plates. Hides in tall grass.

## Ecology

Predator. Hunts [[Fiafhreine (Moss Deer)]] and [[Luch Blatha (Petal Mice)]]. Will prioritise hunting Fiafhreine over attacking the player if any are nearby. In the [[Knotroot Canopy]], appears with more ambush positions and is non-hostile to wildling elves.

## Behaviour

Ambush-based. Hides in tall grass — the grass occasionally shows slight unnatural movement as a tell. If the player approaches, it pounces and chases aggressively. Does not do melee damage directly. Instead, it runs past the player while firing thorn projectiles. When attacked, it spits thorns in all directions. Hostile at all times.

## Attacks & Abilities

- Pounce from concealment
- Thorn spray (omnidirectional, when damaged)
- Thorn projectiles (straight line, while chasing)
- Run-and-gun pattern — runs past the player rather than engaging in melee

## Weaknesses

Predictable pounce timing after the tell. Vulnerable while in pursuit if the player can outmanoeuvre and return fire. The unnatural grass movement is the primary way to detect it before it attacks.

## Visuals

Low, spiny, vine-textured. Bark spine plates. Should blend convincingly into tall grass at a distance. The player needs to learn to watch for the movement tell.

## Audio

Rustling grass (natural vs. unnatural distinction). A sharp crack on pounce. Thorn impacts should sound brittle and organic.

## Design Notes

The Dealgshiul teaches the player to watch their environment before rushing through it. The Fiafhreine's avoidance of tall grass is the first indirect signal that something lives in there. Later, a dead [[Luch Blatha (Petal Mice)]] visible in the grass serves as a more direct warning.
