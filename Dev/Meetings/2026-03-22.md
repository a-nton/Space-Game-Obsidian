# Meeting Minutes — 22 March 2026

**Attendees:** Adrian (Banshi), Kate, Jureto, Borko, Toni (Anton)

---

## Individual Priorities

**Adrian:** Wants a tech demo within six months — a fully playable planet with all mechanics fleshed out and releasable quality. Selling point should be complex character relations, well-written dialogue, and a world that feels alive. Enemy design and level design are strong candidates for the team's focus. Envisions something like Undertale crossed with Blasphemous.

**Kate:** Wants the game to feel like a story where the player is the main character. Puzzle-based, challenging gameplay. Interesting and immersive mechanics. Deep lore comparable to Lord of the Rings. Dialogue and interactions as a primary engagement tool. Art should be a focus alongside atmosphere — colour palette matters.

**Jureto:** Simple, smooth, fun gameplay and animations. Focus on atmosphere. Coherent lore that can be followed without effort but rewards deeper engagement. Prefers tasks tagged by type (R&D, game-ready, bug-fixing) to help with motivation and productivity.

**Borko:** No fixed time limit. One planet first, demo quality. Mechanics should be the priority for the demo. Lore can be developed alongside. Wants the game to be enjoyable to make, not optimised for market success.

**Toni:** Templates and placeholder entities first, so the team can work independently. Focus on procedural effects (lighting, bloom, shadows) rather than hand-drawn asset volume. Ethical dilemmas with multiple endings. Sound design and dialogue as atmosphere carriers. Trippy fourth-wall moments — the game speaking to the player. Reference: Vampire Survivors' Reaper, Blasphemous' atmosphere.

## Design Priority Scoring

The team scored eight categories from 0 to 10. See [[Project Goals#Design Priorities (Team Consensus)]] for full results and definitions.

**Key outcomes:** Atmosphere scored a unanimous 10/10. Dialogue scored 8/10. Art style scored 3/10 — not because it doesn't matter, but because strong colour palettes and post-processing achieve more than high-fidelity assets given the team's resources.

## Decisions Made

- First deliverable: one playable planet at demo quality
- Art style: low pixel count, strong colour palettes, procedural post-processing
- Lore delivery: implied through gameplay and environment, not text-based codex
- Dialogue: high-impact, skippable but rewarding for those who engage
- Atmosphere: the team's unanimous top priority
