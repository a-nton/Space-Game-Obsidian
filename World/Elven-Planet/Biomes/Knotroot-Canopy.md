# Knotroot Canopy

#canon

**Planet:** Elven Planet (Nemethari Homeworld)
**Connects to:** [[Caer Bile]], [[Faeblight Marsh]] (descent)
**Secret boss:** Elder Spider (ascent)
**Progression type:** Horizontal then vertical split

---

## Overview

Dense jungle of entangled living vines, flower-like trees, and moss. Sunlight barely penetrates the thick tree coverage, beaming down only in certain locations. Generally dark, with green lighting and an overgrown dark-forest atmosphere. Water seeps through the soil, creating damp swampy patches and muddy quicksand-like traps.

## Visual Identity

Dark greens and deep shadows. Occasional shafts of golden-green light where the canopy breaks. Bioluminescent fungal growths provide ambient illumination. Damp, dripping, claustrophobic. The contrast with the open Verdant Expanse should be stark.

## Layout & Progression

Terrain is separated into two parts. The first is a long horizontal segment where the player traverses the living jungle. After reaching far enough, the path splits: descend further to reach the [[Faeblight Marsh]], or ascend to fight the Elder Spider (secret miniboss).

## Ecology

Mostly plant-based monsters and mobile creatures. [[Dealgshiul (Thorn Ambusher)]] returns from the Verdant Expanse with more ambush points. [[Portachan Scatha (Mud Shark)]] lurks submerged in mud, detecting vibrations — punishes fast movement. [[Truaillbhas (Fungal Zombie)]] is any creature reanimated by fungal spores, always hostile, releases a spore cloud on death that reanimates nearby corpses. [[Scamaill Lobha (Fungal Cloud)]] drifts slowly and inverts player controls on contact. [[Fiodhreim Mor (Mud Giant)]] is a massive elite that appears when the player causes too much disturbance. [[Creepvine Cluster]] lashes out at fast movement but remains passive if approached slowly.

A secret interaction: if a Fungal Zombie dies over a Mud Shark's corpse in a muddy patch, the infection spreads and the entire swamp transforms into a fungal biome.

## NPCs

A druidic sanctuary inhabited by a reclusive faction of wild Nemethari who live in complete symbiosis with the forest and its plants, coexisting with fungus and speaking for the forest. This provides a way for the player to converse with the nature of the planet. When the civilisation later falls, these wild elves remain true to nature — living proof that elves once lived far longer than the current cycle permits.
