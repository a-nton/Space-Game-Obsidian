# Heartbloom Abattoir

#canon

**Planet:** Elven Planet (Nemethari Homeworld)
**Connects to:** [[The Grove]] (entry only, via catacombs below wildling ziggurat), [[Elderwood Highlands]]
**Progression type:** Horizontal then vertical (traverse the branch, then ascend the main body of the tree)

---

## Overview

A secret area within the world tree — a wooden cavern inside the branch and inside the bark of [[Caer Bile]]. Dark, with ambient lighting from the glowing bark. Inhabited by flora and severely parasitically overgrown fauna. The area contains ritual sacrifice sites, exposing the dark pagan nature of the elder Nemethari traditions.

This is the high-stress bloodbath zone of the planet. All creatures here are hostile toward the player and toward each other. Enemies constantly respawn.

## Visual Identity

Dark wood, glowing bark veins, ritual markings. Organic architecture that feels wrong — sacrificial in nature. The warmth of bioluminescence set against violent, claustrophobic spaces. The area should feel sacred and dangerous simultaneously.

## Ecology

[[Snathfhuil (Bark Crawler)]] — small root-like creatures that travel through bark walls and emerge for quick stab attacks before retreating. [[Iobartach Coirt (Bark Juggernaut)]] — slow, massively durable humanoid fused with bark and ritual growth. Explodes into root clusters on death. [[Sap Horror]] — large quadrupedal sap creature, hunted by everything else despite being the dominant predator. [[Fiodhreime Remnant (Bark Slime)]] — fragmented root masses that lunge unpredictably, appear in large numbers.

All creatures prioritise attacking each other over the player, making stealth possible but dangerous — the moment there is no other target, everything aggros the player.

[[Bark Mites]] consume corpses, preventing area clutter. [[Root Worms]] serve as ambient critters and a capturable resource.

## Lore Significance

Contains clues that Caer Bile is not a natural construct — materials that do not match any known Nemethari craft, structural geometry that follows engineering principles rather than organic growth, and data fragments in a language the player's translation matrix cannot fully parse. These point toward an [[The Accord|Accord]]-era origin.

Primary function as a game space is the connection between [[The Grove]] and the [[Elderwood Highlands]].
