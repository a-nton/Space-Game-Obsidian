# Elderwood Highlands

#canon

**Planet:** Elven Planet (Nemethari Homeworld)
**Connects to:** [[Heartbloom Abattoir]] (entry), [[Caer Bile]] (one-way shortcut out after obtaining material)
**Progression type:** Open, stealth-focused

---

## Overview

The crown of the [[Caer Bile]] tree. A flat, open garden of serene luminous petals, exposed to the sky. The area is lit by the petals and by the unobstructed sky above — in which the sleeping [[The Beholder|Beholder]] is visible.

This is where the player finds the material required to upgrade their ship — the initial reason they came to this planet.

## Visual Identity

Bright, ethereal, unsettling. Luminous petals in soft pastel tones against the open sky. The beauty is genuine and the danger is absolute. The sky above is unobstructed, and the Beholder reads as a region where the stars do not behave correctly — light bending, colour draining away. Proximity should produce the Beholder's subsonic pressure effect and trigger UI anomalies.

## Gameplay

The area encourages stealth and running rather than fighting. Flora enemies are giant and lethal.

[[Blathri Mor (Petal Giant)]] — massive walking flower colossi that patrol the area in large numbers. Technically killable but heavily discouraged. Core mechanic: periodically all Petal Giants stop, hold hands, and begin singing. When the music quiets down, they stop and look around — the player must hide. If spotted, they attack and kill instantly. A red-light-green-light game.

[[Speirphudar (Pollen Floater)]] — semi-visible floating pollen entity. Detects fast movement. Releases a focused pollen burst if the player moves too quickly nearby. Evasive and unreliable to fight.

[[Petal Drifters]], [[Sky Moths]], and [[Seed Dancers]] serve as ambient non-hostile fauna. The Seed Dancers sing along when the Petal Giants hold hands.

## The Beholder

The sky above the Highlands is unobstructed, making this the only location on the elf planet where the Beholder is directly visible. It does not have a clear shape — it reads as an eldritch presence. Standing here in the open should produce subsonic pressure, tonal distortion, and unexplained UI anomalies.

This is the probable location for the [[The Recursive Observer Stack|Recursive Observer Stack]] moment — the formal disruption where the player suspects the game knows they are there.
