# Faeblight Marsh

#canon

**Planet:** Elven Planet (Nemethari Homeworld)
**Connects to:** [[Knotroot Canopy]]
**One-way shortcut to:** [[Caer Bile]]
**Progression type:** Platforming

---

## Overview

A light-blue ambient underground cavern with a serene glowing river flowing through it. The gameplay space consists of platforming above the river. Beautiful and lethal — the water itself is the primary threat.

## Visual Identity

Bioluminescent blue tones. The river glows softly, casting rippling light on the cavern ceiling. Serene atmosphere undercut by constant danger. The contrast between the tranquil visuals and the lethal water creates tension.

## Layout & Progression

Platforming across surfaces above the river. Falling in means near-instant death via the [[Oillipheist Faebhrigh (Great Serpent)]]. An item obtainable on another planet allows the player to move through water without creating disturbance, enabling them to sneak past the Leviathan and reach the lake bottom.

At the bottom: submerged structures clearly not built by the Nemethari. Their architectural style matches a different Accord species, suggesting this cavern predates the current civilisation. The Leviathan itself closely resembles the most common pets on the fish planet — hinting it was a scientist's pet, abandoned when the Accord fell.

This is the first cross-planet Metroidvania gate in the game. It establishes the principle that planetary content can be revisited with tools gained elsewhere.

## Ecology

[[Gloineach Leimneach (Jumpfish)]] leap from the water in targeted arcs — the primary active threat while platforming. [[Foscar Tonn (Ambush Lily)]] hides beneath the surface and spikes upward when triggered. [[Crab Portach (Dwell Crab)]] moves along shallow edges — juveniles are passive, adults at the lake bottom are hostile. [[Taibhse Srutha (Wisp)]] drifts as ambient bioluminescence, can be captured as a resource. [[Each Uisce (Sea Horse)]] stands partially submerged near banks — neutral unless provoked, then extremely aggressive. The Great Serpent defines the entire biome's movement rules.

## NPCs

Dead fish people's corpses. Environmental storytelling only — no living NPCs.

## Cross-Planet Gate

The submerged structures at the lake bottom require the silent-movement-in-water item from another planet. This gate must recur on other planets to avoid feeling like a one-off.
