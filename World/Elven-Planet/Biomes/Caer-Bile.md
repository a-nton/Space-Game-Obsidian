## Overview

The world tree. A colossal living structure housing the Nemethari main city. The area looks like a gigantic living tree with branching platforms and openings that can be entered. The central hub of the elven planet — all major routes pass through or connect to Caer Bile.

## Visual Identity

Massive organic architecture. Bark, sap veins, bioluminescent growths. Lower sections are warmer and more populated. Upper sections are darker, more overgrown, and increasingly hostile. The tree should feel alive — creaking, breathing, dripping sap.

## Layout & Progression

Progression is primarily vertical. Branching platforms, interior chambers, ascending paths. Lower sections host the city and smaller fauna-based enemies. Upper sections feature larger flora-based threats. The transition from civilised lower city to wild upper canopy should be gradual and atmospheric.

## Hidden Origin

Caer Bile is not a natural tree. The [[Heartbloom Abattoir]] contains evidence that it was originally an Accord scientific installation — a biological megastructure designed for a purpose that predates the Nemethari civilisation's memory. The [[Nemethari Elder]] may have reshaped it when he constructed his camouflage world, or it may have already been growing when he found the system.

## Ecology

**Lower sections:** [[Rithroisin (Sap Bug)]] swarm along bark surfaces. [[Braonan (Parasite Slug)]] attaches to anything that walks over it. [[Scathchoirt (Bark Chameleon)]] hunts from camouflage on vertical surfaces.

**Mid sections:** [[Coimeadai Roisin (Root Sentinel)]] patrols sap veins and becomes aggressive if sap nodes are damaged. [[Sciathan Darach (Blossom Eagle)]] swoops from above — a timed audio-cue stealth mechanic where the player must find cover within 3 seconds of hearing it.

**Upper sections:** [[Daingnean Choroine (Vine Obstructor Cluster)]] blocks paths with vine barriers. [[Lochrann Spoir (Smoke Orchid)]] releases blinding spore bursts.

[[Leimfhear (Glowbug)]] appears here in an amber-coloured variant. [[Luch Blatha (Petal Mice)]] hide in bark crevices.

## Design Notes

The Blossom Eagle audio-cue mechanic (hear sound → find cover within 3 seconds or die) is introduced to the player by watching the eagle kill a parasite slug first. This teaches the mechanic through environmental storytelling before the player is at risk.
