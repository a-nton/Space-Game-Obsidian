# The Elder of the Dechrai

#canon

**Planet:** [[The Accord|Elder's Planet]] (Athnemeton)
**Faction:** [[Dechrai]]
**Ascension candidate:** Yes (forced only)

---

## Overview

The oldest living creature on the planet. He remembers things no one else does. Elder of the Dechrai camps, highly respected by his people and treated as their leader. Compassionate and fair to his kind, but cold toward the [[Cethrai]] — not toward the individuals, but toward the system that binds them.

## Personality & Worldview

He does not hate the Cethrai themselves. He despises the deity, believing that forced reliance on the energy cycle suppresses his people's potential. He has watched generations of Dechrai hunted and killed for the crime of existing outside the cycle. That has made him patient, strategic, and deeply angry in a way that does not announce itself.

He knows practically everything about the world and its history. He is the living archive. Speaking with him is the primary way the player accesses deep historical context.

## Relationship with the Player

If the player allies with him, he provides support, information, and reveals his ambition: to overthrow the [[The High Priest|High Priest]] and forcefully disconnect all Cethrai, believing they would eventually thank him once they realised how much freer being Dechrai makes you.

## Arc — Before the Invasion

Leader of the Dechrai camps. Survives in the margins, shelters his people, plans quietly.

## Arc — During the Invasion

He finds morbid satisfaction in the chaos. The Cethrai's cycle is failing without his intervention. He seems aware that the god at the core is dead (or close to it), but he is ignorant of the parasites' true destructive potential — that left unchecked, they would hollow out the planet entirely.

If informed and allied with, he provides support to the player in clearing the infestation.

## Arc — After the Tunnels Are Cleared

He feels he has succeeded. If told about the ascension blade, he dismisses it as myth and expresses strong disapproval toward creating a new god. If pressed, he shows signs of hostility toward the player. The last thing he wants is for the cycle to start again.

## Ascension Outcome

He refuses. There is the option to forcefully stab him with the blade. His ascension succeeds but produces a deity consumed by rage. He has spent thousands of years watching his people hunted, marginalised, and killed. That fury, compressed and amplified into divine cognition, detonates. The planet convulses. The Cethrai transform into hunted creatures. The world becomes a mirror of the suffering the Dechrai endured, inverted and made absolute.

**Theological parallel:** The Old Testament flood. Shiva as destroyer. The rage is earned, the destruction is understandable, and the world dies anyway. Of all four endings, this one commits the hardest to its consequences.

**Societal outcome:** Collapse. The anointed mutate into monsters and murder each other indiscriminately.
