# The Rebel

#canon

**Planet:** [[The Accord|Elder's Planet]] (Athnemeton)
**Faction:** [[Cethrai]] → [[Dechrai]] (self-severed)
**Ascension candidate:** Yes
**Recruitable companion:** Possibly

---

## Overview

A young Cethrai who leads a dissident movement advocating for the Dechrai's reintegration into society. She is the adopted daughter of the [[The High Priest|High Priest]], which grants her a degree of protection from the persecution her views would otherwise invite. The only character respected in both communities, though fully trusted by neither.

## Personality & Worldview

She questions the rightfulness of the mainstream church. She leads a group of individuals viewed as heretical, who believe the Dechrai deserve to be treated with the same respect as anyone else. The face and icon of their organisation is a Cethrai who lost his soul-gem in battle while defending the city — evidence that not all Dechrai became so by choice.

She constantly tries to recruit the player for her cause, and unsuccessfully attempts to rally people and build shelters for the Dechrai.

## Relationship with the Player

Eager, persistent, idealistic. She wants the player on her side and is not subtle about it. She sees the player as someone outside the system who might actually help change it.

## Arc — Before the Invasion

She campaigns. She fails. She is tolerated because of her adoptive father's position, but her movement gains no real traction. The system is too comfortable for most Cethrai to question.

## Arc — During the Invasion

She resists parasitic possession longer than any other Cethrai. In a desperate act, she tears her own crystal from her chest, becoming Dechrai voluntarily. This is the single most transgressive act possible in her society. She allies with the [[The Elder of the Dechrai|Elder of the Dechrai]], believing the crisis proves her movement was right all along.

## Arc — After the Tunnels Are Cleared

She is conflicted with the new knowledge but moves on — she believes it falls to her to lead her now-depressed people. She volunteers to be ascended upon learning about the blade.

## Ascension Outcome

Her goal is to unify. She dissolves the distinction between Cethrai and Dechrai. Everyone is reconnected to the cycle on equal terms. No one is killed on sight. No one is an outcast.

But rebellion requires something to rebel against, and a god who enforces equality from a position of absolute power has eliminated the conditions that produced her own values. Her world is peaceful. Her world is also a place where no one can want anything that would require wanting differently from anyone else. The paradise is comfortable and total, and it permits no friction — and friction was the thing that made her who she was.

**Theological parallel:** Closer to Huxley's *Brave New World* than Orwell's *1984*. Nobody is punished because nobody can want anything that would require punishment. The Rebel's ending is the game's statement that even the good ideology, when handed divine power, becomes indistinguishable from the others.

**Societal outcome:** Superficially utopian. Deep stagnation. Logistical problems accumulate because the focus is on unity rather than function. No one is oppressed. No one is free in any way that matters.
