# The Scholar

#canon

**Planet:** [[The Accord|Elder's Planet]] (Athnemeton)
**Faction:** [[Cethrai]] (altered)
**Ascension candidate:** Yes
**Recruitable companion:** Yes

---

## Overview

A Cethrai researcher who has been experimenting on her own soul-gem, partially severing and reconnecting it to study the energy cycle from the inside. This has left her physically altered — neither fully Cethrai nor fully Dechrai. She occupies the position between the two. Technically still connected to the cycle, but her crystal is damaged, and with it, something in the way she processes other people's suffering has been attenuated.

## Personality & Worldview

Her moral complexity comes from a genuine inability to weigh suffering against knowledge. She does not enjoy causing harm. She simply does not register it as a relevant variable. When she runs tests on living subjects, she records distress the way she records temperature — as data that might be useful later. She is dangerous in a way that is difficult to argue with, because she is usually right about the facts and consistently wrong about what the facts should be used for.

Treated with some degree of respect by the Cethrai but shunned as a weirdo. Despised by the Dechrai for abducting their children and experimenting on them — symbolised as a devil figure in Dechrai culture.

## Relationship with the Player

She gives morally questionable quests — injecting bystanders (both Cethrai and Dechrai) with suspicious serums and reporting the results. After the tunnels are cleared and proof of the Elder's death is provided, she moves into the caverns and begins running tests on the god's remains.

She is the one who discovers that the god was actually an ascended alien. She provides the player with genetic data that can be analysed by the ship to locate the original Nemethari homeworld — the clue for where to find the ascension blade.

She can be recruited as a companion after this point, providing ship upgrades and analytical support.

## Arc — During the Invasion

She does not fall to parasitic possession due to how tampered her connection already is. She still feels something is wrong. She tasks the player with investigating the tunnels initially.

## Ascension Outcome

She agrees readily. "For science." She becomes a deity of pure inquiry. The world does not become cruel in any deliberate way. Suffering is not inflicted. But suffering is also not treated as a problem worth solving. The civilisation advances rapidly in knowledge and technology but loses warmth, loses investment in one another, loses the sense that a life has weight beyond the data it generates.

The Scholar is the only ascended deity who retains her individual memories, because her analytical mind structures information too rigidly to dissolve into the collective field. She remembers everything. She cares about none of it.

**Theological parallel:** The Enlightenment's shadow — the idea that sufficient knowledge will produce sufficient goodness, shown to be hollow. Her retention of individual memory while losing empathy is an inversion of the Buddhist concept of enlightenment, where the dissolution of self is supposed to produce compassion. She achieves retention without compassion.

**Societal outcome:** Dystopian sci-fi. Rapid technological development. Ethics do not exist. Human experimentation is praised.
