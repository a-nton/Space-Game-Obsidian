# The High Priest

#canon

**Planet:** [[The Accord|Elder's Planet]] (Athnemeton)
**Faction:** [[Cethrai]]
**Ascension candidate:** Yes

---

## Overview

Leader of the dominant religious institution and sovereign of the major cities. His faith is absolute, legalistic, and unyielding. He leads the persecution of the [[Dechrai]], viewing their existence as corruption. He treats the player with cold tolerance — useful enough to keep around, but too foreign to trust.

## Personality & Worldview

Old Testament lawful neutral. He does not question the system because the system is the god, and the god is everything. Deeply cold, deeply certain. His strength and his blindness are the same quality: absolute conviction. He is not stupid — he is committed, and commitment of this kind makes certain kinds of intelligence inaccessible.

## Relationship with the Player

Not hostile, but not willing to help. The player is not part of the world and therefore not part of the plan. He tolerates their presence as long as it does not threaten the order he maintains.

## Arc — Before the Invasion

The unchallenged sovereign. He leads the movement against the Dechrai, framing the killing of the severed as a righteous, even merciful act. Society functions under his authority because no one has reason to question it yet.

## Arc — During the Invasion

The first and most prominent figure to fall to the [[Sreng|parasitic]] corruption. His faith makes him incapable of acknowledging that anything could compromise his god. His crystal tints blue-purple. He does not notice because noticing would require doubting.

## Arc — After the Tunnels Are Cleared

Released from possession, he still refuses to accept the [[Nemethari Elder|Elder's]] death. He constructs an alternative narrative: the god has retreated, the faithful have failed a test, and the answer is more devotion. He begins moving toward authoritarian control.

If shown proof of the Elder's death, he suppresses it. He decides the Cethrai cannot survive this knowledge. He bans all mention of the fact and doubles down on the narrative of spiritual failure.

## Ascension Outcome

He accepts without hesitation. He has spent his entire life serving a god, and now he can become one. He does not pause to consider whether he should, because he has already decided, in the moment he suppressed the truth, that he is the only one fit to hold this power.

His ascension produces a deity of law, order, and material provision. The world functions. Nobody suffers physically. Nobody is free. The cities flourish. The people are slaves. The Dechrai are fully eradicated.

**Theological parallel:** Dostoevsky's Grand Inquisitor. "We have corrected Thy work. We have founded it upon miracle, mystery, and authority." The suppression of truth is the Church's historical justification for controlling scripture, and his godhood is what happens when that justification is given teeth.

**Societal outcome:** Authoritarian modern-day. Efficient, cold, closed.
