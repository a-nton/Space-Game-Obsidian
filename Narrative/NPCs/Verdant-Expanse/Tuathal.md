# Túathal

#canon

**Location:** [[Verdant Expanse]] — village
**Faction:** [[Nemethari Culture|Nemethari]] (village elf)
**Role:** Lore source, quest pointer
**Name meaning:** "Ruler of the people" — from Old Irish *túath* (people, country) + *fal* (rule)

---

## Overview

The village elder. Knowledgeable, protective of his people, and deeply connected to the forest. He carries the practical knowledge that complements [[Aine]]'s mythological knowledge.

## Personality

Patient, measured, sceptical of anything he cannot verify through experience. He loves his villagers and takes his responsibility seriously. He is not closed-minded — he simply requires evidence before he will commit to a belief. This makes him a reliable source of grounded information and a counterpoint to the more superstitious elements of elven culture.

## Function

- Knows about the secret area in the Verdant Expanse and can point the player toward it
- Provides information on the [[Nemethari Culture|elven connection with the forest]] and their lifespan's relationship to that connection
- Dismisses the [[Oillipheist Faebhrigh (Great Serpent)|Leviathan]] as myth — creating a productive disagreement with [[Aine]]
- Can provide context for the abandoned Alliance laboratory if the player discovers it and returns

## Key Dialogue Beats

When asked about the forest, he speaks with genuine reverence — not religious, but ecological. He understands the symbiosis at a practical level. When asked about the Leviathan, he is dismissive but not contemptuous. He has lived long enough to know that most legends are exaggerations of real things, but he has not seen this particular real thing.

If the player finds the Alliance laboratory and describes it to him, he is troubled. He cannot explain it. This is one of the few moments where his confidence falters.

## Relationships

- Contrast with [[Aine]] on the Leviathan question
- Respected by [[Mathgamain]] as a peer
- The village children trust him but find him boring compared to Áine's stories
