# Nemethari Culture

#canon

---

The Nemethari are the original species from which the [[Nemethari Elder]] came. Their culture is the source material from which the [[Athnemeton]] world was imperfectly reconstructed.

## Appearance

Humanoid with plant features: bark-textured skin, leaf-like hair, bioluminescent markings. They wear elaborate jewellery as indicators of social role — and these adornments are separate from their bodies, worn and removed deliberately. This is the key visual distinction between them and the Athnemeton, where jewellery has fused into the flesh.

Based on the Sidhe of Irish folklore — fairies that look like dryads, with both plant and humanoid features and regal accessories.

## Society

Chieftain-type monarchy. The king must be the most connected with nature, but wise enough to resist being consumed by it. Society is built around the monarchy structure, but the king's authority derives from his symbiotic bond with the natural world.

The modern king does not have as strong a connection with nature as he should — which contributes to the later disaster of industrialisation. He is the son of the ascended king (the [[Nemethari Elder]], now the god living beneath another civilisation's planet), and was given the right to reign on that basis. Kings are perceived as god-like.

## Time & Ritual

Time and life are cyclical. The four great festivals — Samhain, Imbolc, Beltane, Lughnasadh — mark the harvest cycles. Society is deeply ritualistic. The rituals serve a purpose, rooted in symbiosis with the natural world. This is in contrast to the Athnemeton, where rituals persist but their reasons are lost.

## Relationship with Nature

Largely symbiotic. The Nemethari worship the power of fauna — chaotic, self-willed, caring only about balance. Their architecture grows from the landscape: houses carved into living trees, communities formed under earthen mounds, cities resembling forest encampments. Certain predatory spirits are hunted in seasonal rituals — respected rather than feared.

## Ethnoses

- **Village elves** — civilisation, the [[Verdant Expanse]]
- **City elves** — civilisation, [[Caer Bile]]
- **Druidic elves** — reclusive, the [[Knotroot Canopy]]
- **Wildling elves** — [[The Grove]] & [[Heartbloom Abattoir]]
- **Fish people** — extinct, the [[Faeblight Marsh]]

## The Overgrown

To be "overgrown" is considered a disease — an unhealthy symbiotic state in which rational thought is lost. Only the smartest elves can remain sane while overgrown. Overgrown elves either run to the Grove themselves or become outcasts. The first civilised elf to become overgrown was the Elder of the Grove, who was feared and exiled. He was intelligent enough to manipulate the wild elves into following his rule and works to maintain the balance between them and civilised society.

## Mythology & Prejudices

- Don't go to the Knotroot Canopy, or the Elder monster will eat you (a distorted memory of the Leviathan; in truth only the Elder Spider is there)
- Myth about Flora living in the sky (actually Flora's party in the [[Elderwood Highlands]])
- Myth about modern elves appearing from the ground-waters (a remnant of the fish people)
- A bedtime story about a scary grove king who steals children in their sleep (a remnant of the Elder Elf, the first overgrown)

## Figures of Speech

- "Grovers!" — profanity, equivalent to "fuckers"
- "One overgrown man's troubles are worth whole Fauna's days" — overgrown = evil

## Food

Animal food, since plants are sacred.

## Weapons

Utensil-weapons (hammers and similar). The only standing army is stationed in Caer Bile, near the Grove.

## The Outgrowing Revolution

Later in the planet's history, the negative connotation attached to the "grovers" creates a cultural drift between fauna and elves, leading to the exploitation of fauna through industrialised science. This period is called the Outgrowing Revolution — the elven equivalent of an industrial revolution. It represents the civilisation's fall from symbiosis into extraction.

The Nemethari developed interstellar travel via plant-symbiosis, through which they met the other species of the [[The Accord|Alliance]]. They donated a planet's biome to the fish people and their Leviathan.
