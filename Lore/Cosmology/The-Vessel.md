# The Vessel (Player Character)

#canon

---

The Vessel is a biomechanical construct originally designed by all six species as a housing shell for the [[Aeonic Drive]]. It was built to integrate organic and synthetic systems so that it could interface with every Discipline simultaneously. It carries defining traits from each of the six species — a convergence of six engineering traditions in one body.

After the [[The Accord|Accord]] fell, the [[The Beholder|Beholder]] repurposed it as the command unit for its extraction fleet. When the Beholder entered hibernation, the Vessel lost power and drifted through empty space.

## Reactivation

The Vessel eventually collided with an asteroid that once hosted an Accord-era spacecraft testing facility. It lay dormant there until a small creature from the [[Nemethari Elder|Elder's]] planet wandered into the cave where it had come to rest. The creature carried a crystal linking it to the planet's energy cycle, and when it died near the Vessel, the crystal's residual energy triggered reactivation.

The Amplification Core, shattered by the Elder's final act, lay in fragments at the far end of the cave. The Vessel integrated the largest remaining shard. This fragment retained the Core's essential property: it amplifies any energy it receives. Fed by even a trace of the planet's life-field, the shard produces enough power to sustain the Vessel indefinitely.

The Vessel found an abandoned ship at the testing facility and used the Core shard to power it.

## The Player's Goal

Collect all six [[Aeonic Drive]] Disciplines from across the galaxy, stabilise them with a seventh component, and use the completed Drive to confront the Beholder.

The seventh component is the Vessel itself. See [[Aeonic Drive#The Seventh Component]].

## Visual Design

The body should reflect its multi-species origin: organic textures alongside machined surfaces, six distinct material signatures layered into a single chassis. Its biomechanical nature explains why it can absorb both technological energy and organic crystal energy — it was engineered as a universal interface.
