# The Overseers

#canon

---

The [[The Beholder|Beholder]] selected one representative from each species and enslaved them. These six, now called the Overseers, were forced to remain within their home systems. Their role was to subtly sabotage their own civilisations: suppressing rediscovery, discouraging ambition, misdirecting intellectual progress. Each Overseer retained enough of their original intelligence to be effective, and enough memory to understand exactly what they had lost.

## Gnostic Parallel

The Overseers function the way Archons function in Gnostic cosmology. In the Sethian and Valentinian systems, the Archons are servants of the Demiurge stationed at each celestial sphere, and their task is to prevent souls from ascending past their level. The six Overseers, each bound to their home system, each tasked with sabotaging their own civilisation's development, perform this function exactly. They guard the Disciplines the way Archons guard the gates.

Like the Archons in many Gnostic texts, the Overseers are not alien invaders. They are corrupted emanations of the divine, turned against their own nature. There is a deliberate echo between "beholding" and "overseeing."

## The Beholder's Hibernation

After the Beholder consumed the Accord's Energy Crystal Vault, the immense power overloaded it, forcing it into a brief dormancy. This sleep released the six Overseers from its control.

Most of the freed Overseers collapsed into despair upon realising how thoroughly their civilisations had been dismantled. One of them — the [[Nemethari Elder]] — acted.

The Beholder's hibernation also severed its hold on the robotic extraction army. Without direction, the Overseers lost their memories. They retained only fragmented, dream-like impressions of what they had once been. Some robotic units shut down entirely, including [[The Vessel]]. Others remained active but directionless, wandering their assigned planets as autonomous remnants.
