# The Nemethari Elder

#canon

---

One of the six [[The Overseers|Overseers]] enslaved by the [[The Beholder|Beholder]]. When the Beholder entered hibernation and released its hold, most of the freed Overseers collapsed into despair. The Nemethari Elder acted.

## Ascension

He located his species' contribution to the [[Aeonic Drive]] — the Amplification Core. This was the component designed to magnify any energy input by orders of magnitude. He used it on himself. The Core was never meant to interface with a living body. It mutated him, expanding his physical and cognitive capacities far beyond biological limits. His perception reached across his local system. His cognition could reshape matter within a limited radius. His lifespan extended past any meaningful constraint.

The civilisation he later created would call him a god. He would not have used that word.

## The Constructed Planet

The Beholder would wake soon, and the Elder's amplified mind was now the loudest cognitive signal in the galaxy. To survive, he needed to become invisible. The only way to hide a mind that bright was to break it into pieces and scatter it.

He constructed a planet from raw materials available in his system, seeded it with life derived from his memories of the Nemethari homeworld, and linked every living creature to a shared energy cycle. Each creature functioned as a node in a distributed network that scattered and masked the Elder's signal.

The hundred-year recycling cap on each creature's lifespan served the same purpose. If any single node accumulated too much energy for too long, it would begin to emit a detectable spike. The recycling prevented this. In practice, this is samsara as engineering.

## The Demiurge Parallel

The Elder is close to a Platonic Demiurge: he shapes the material world from imperfect memory of a higher reality. His planet is a copy of a copy. The [[Athnemeton]] themselves are shadows on a cave wall — literally Plato's allegory applied to world-building. The fused jewellery, the rituals without meaning, the saturated colours are all symptoms of a degraded emanation.

There is also a parallel to the Kabbalistic concept of the Shevirat HaKelim (the shattering of the vessels), where divine light pours into forms that cannot quite hold it and the result is a world that is beautiful and broken at the same time.

## Death

The Beholder eventually tracked the Elder through [[The Vessel]], which it sent to investigate. The Elder, sensing the Vessel's approach and not knowing it was now free of the Beholder's control, attacked it in a final act of self-preservation. He destroyed the only thing that might have saved him, because by the time it reached him he had been afraid for too long to recognise help when it came.

Frame this as tragedy rather than villainy. The Elder's attack on the Vessel is fear, not malice. He is the architect of a world and the prisoner of the same world's logic.

Now only a husk remains at the planet's core, drained by the [[Sreng|parasites]].
